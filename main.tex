\begin{abstract}

    \textbf{Background:} Modern Large Language Models (LLMs) enable code
    generation from natural language but perform inconsistently across languages
    due to variations in training data and linguistic features. Tamil often
    underperforms compared to other languages, potentially due to differences
    between standardized literary Tamil and less standardized colloquial Tamil,
    as well as the impact of code-mixing with English. Understanding the
    shortcomings of the current generation of LLMs in successfully generating code from 
    Tamil descriptions is essential in guiding their development, fine tuning,
    and use, particularly in educational settings.\\
    \vspace{0.25em}\\
    \noindent\textbf{Objective:} This study aims to investigate the differences
    in how novices and experts articulate computational tasks in Tamil, as well
    as to analyze the linguistic characteristics of responses that succeed or
    fail in generating code through an LLM.\\
    \vspace{0.25em}\\
    \noindent\textbf{Methods:} We perform think-aloud interviews with
    computer science instructors and novices literate in Tamil while completing
    Explain-in-Plain-Language (EiPL) questions, where participants provide
    prompts to generate functionally equivalent code. The recorded interviews
    will be transcribed and analyzed using thematic coding, focusing on
    code-mixing and the use of colloquial (kondun) versus (sen) literary Tamil.\\

\end{abstract}
